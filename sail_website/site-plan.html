About Sailing

Sailing has been a part of the human experience for century’s, in fact some of the earliest art ever discovered pictured sailboats. Every major civilization sailed and even had their own unique sail boats. Some of these are familiar to you, the Viking longboat, the Polynesian Voyaging Canoe, and of course the Spanish Galleon. The traditions of sailing are still strong in the coastal regions of most countries, but with the advance of transportation sailing is spreading inland. Now sailing is a common sight on some of Idaho’s lakes and reservoirs. You may have noticed driving past American Falls reservoir some large colorful sails skimming the water, just a few years ago that would have seemed out of place, but today it is a more common occurrence.

Why Try Sailing

Sailing is a common tie most cultures have one to another, it is a shared experience that two cultures can share. Not to mention that it is incredibly fun. Depending on what you are interested in there is a type of sailing for that! Want a fast-paced adventure? Try racing sailing. Just want to explore? Just do some exploration sailing. Looking for a relaxing time with friends? You can just let the wind guide your sail and have the time of your life. There are numerous types of sailboats, but the most common ones to see in southeast Idaho would be the catamaran and the sloop. Catamarans have good stability, and can go really fast, this type of sailboat has two hulls connected by beams in between covered by a trampoline type floor. For advanced sailors, the Catamaran can be a thrilling ride. There are multiple kinds of sloops, we have the standard sloop. A great choice for beginners to learn the ropes of sailing and to become more comfortable on the water. This boat is also good for hanging out with a few friends and having a good time. Then we have the racing sloop, this boat can be one of the fastest sailboats in southeast Idaho. The single hull design helps the boat cut through the water with ease. This type of sailboat is best used by those who have done sailing for a bit as the design cuts stability for that increase of speed.
